Tips and Tricks for College Students!

Necessary Equipment

  • A desktop computer or laptop.
  • For a Windows computer, you need at least Windows 7, with an Intel Pentium 4 processor or better. (The Intel Pentium 4 was released in 2001 for reference)
  • For a Macbook, you will need OS X El Capitan 10.11. (OS X EL Capitan 10.11 is installed on most Macbooks made after 2015.)
  • A reliable internet connection.
  • If your major involves engineering, computer science, art, or any other major involving a lot of computer work, make sure your computer meets the minimum specs for software like Photoshop or modelling software.

Digital Literacy

Necessary Skills
  • Be able to browse the internet.
  • Know how to read and send emails.
  • Know how to use cloud storage like Google Drive.
  • Know how to submit a file for an assignment.
  • Print and scan files.
  • Know how to join a Zoom call with video and microphone enabled.
  • Know how to use a library website to find resources for a paper.
  • Create PowerPoints and be able to present them.

Social Interaction

  • All college courses are structure very differently. Some will have manditory Zoom meetings multiple times a week, while others will only involve watching a teacher's lecture videos.
  • Some students like having only video lectures, and hate Zoom meetings. Some are the opposite. If you dislike video lectures, look for online classes with weekling meetings, or hybrid courses.
  • Many colleges offer online tutoring for many majors or mandatory courses. Check your schools website to see what resources are available to you.
  • Do not be afraid to reach out to your friends or teachers during online school. These are stressful times for everyone, and someone will be willing to help!

Home Environment

  • Try to create an environment similar to school when you work.
  • Taking the time to get ready for "school" can help you to form a routine. This can increase productivity.
  • Working in a quiet environment with limited distractions will also help you to be more productive.
  • If you are ever feeling burnt out, manage your time so you can take breaks to relax, or spend time with family.
  • Consider driving or getting a ride somewhere with free Wifi, like Starbucks or McDonald's.

Asynchronous Learning

  • Asynchronous learning allows a lot of flexibility, but also takes a lot of responsibility.
  • Use a planner or an online calendar to record when you have meetings, or assignments due.
  • It is much better to get a head start on assignments than it is to wait for the due date. Doing a bit of work everyday can make a project much more managable.
  • If you are really worried about asynchronous learning, see if your school offers hybrid classes. These are usually half online, half in person.
  • Online schooling can actually open up your work schedule, as your work is more flexibile you are able to plan around school better.
